🎧 Headphone Power Calculator

Calculate power requirements for any headphone with optional EQ boost

Step 1 — Sensitivity

Most reliable. From AudioScienceReview's sensitivity chart. Lower mV = more sensitive.

e.g. "94 dB/mW". The reference is 1mW — voltage for that 1mW depends on impedance, so always pair with the correct impedance figure below.

Used by some brands (e.g. Audeze). Fixed 1V reference regardless of impedance.

Step 2 — Impedance

Use the lowest impedance between the listed spec and the ASR measurement — it gives the higher (safer) power figure.

110 dB SPL is the standard reference for transient headroom calculations.

Step 3 — EQ Boost

Only the single largest positive gain filter matters — it sets the worst-case power multiplier.

Calculation Steps

1. Get mW at sensitivity SPL
Convert sensitivity to a power value at the reference SPL. For measured mV @ 94 dB SPL: P = V² ÷ R. For dB/mW: 1 mW by definition. For dB/V: P = 1 ÷ R.

2. Scale to target SPL (power ratio)
Power ratio = 10^((targetSPL − sensitivitySPL) ÷ 10)
Base power at target SPL = baseline mW × power ratio

3. Apply EQ multiplier
EQ multiplier = 10^(boost_dB ÷ 10)
Final power = base power × EQ multiplier

Why measured sensitivity is preferred
Manufacturer dB/mW figures often overstate efficiency. A measured mV value from an independent source (e.g. ASR) reflects the headphone's actual voltage requirement to reach 94 dB SPL, leading to a more realistic power figure.
